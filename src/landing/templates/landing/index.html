Hello!

Would it be weird if that wasn't a picture of me?

Yes. But I've been living in Austin long enough to know that being weird is often encouraged. Especially after moving here from Florida. Yet, ironically, I had never heard of the state's "strange" stereotype until moving out of it. I wouldn't say this off-beat background led me to my current path, but I'm sure it helped.

In the summer of 2016 I began building a tiny house on wheels with my friend while studying independently to become a software engineer - Python being my weapon of choice. Each has brought on its own challenges. From implementing electrical wiring to understanding the command line, I've learned a lot. And it's not just building, but designing, problem solving, and managing personal projects that has exercised some of my deepest creative abilities to solve the inherent challenges of engineering something from scratch.

Beyond these long term endeavors I do somehow manage a normal life. I love sleep because it's like a time machine to breakfast. I enjoy reading, cooking, and practicing yoga and meditation in my free time and stay up too late when I don't have free time. Hiking to hidden water spots and travelling to see family has also been very rewarding since moving to Austin in the Fall of 2014.


My Goal for this blog...

Is to teach and illustrate what I've learned. Whether it's a how-to guide or a story or otherwise, the narrative will be rich and informative. I'd like to help those just getting started in a format that would have been useful for the beginner that's still inside of me.
